II. Observations · 观察Observations
Obs. 1

遗忘先快后慢Forgetting is Front-Loaded

艾宾浩斯实验（无意义音节）显示：1 小时内大量遗忘，但 1 天后速度大幅减缓。有意义的内容遗忘更慢，但规律相同——及时的第一轮复习最关键。Ebbinghaus's experiment (nonsense syllables) shows massive forgetting within 1 hour, but the rate slows dramatically after 1 day. Meaningful content decays slower, but the pattern holds — the first review is the most critical.

Obs. 2

间隔 > 连续Spacing > Massing

同样的学习时间，分散在 10 天比集中在 1 天效果好 2-3 倍。连续阅读制造的是"熟悉感幻觉"。The same study time spread over 10 days is 2-3× more effective than crammed into 1 day. Continuous reading creates an "illusion of fluency."

Obs. 3

提取 > 重读Retrieval > Rereading

主动回忆（合上书本自测）比被动重读效率显著更高。测试不是检测工具，是学习工具——它在"困难模式"下强化神经连接。Active recall (self-testing with the book closed) is significantly more effective than passive rereading. Testing is a learning tool, not just assessment — it strengthens neural connections in "hard mode."

Obs. 4

间隔要「刚好忘」Review at the Edge

在"快忘但没全忘"时复习，记忆强化效果最佳。太频繁浪费精力，太稀疏前功尽弃。Reviewing when you're "about to forget but haven't yet" yields the strongest memory consolidation. Too frequent wastes effort; too sparse loses progress.

III. Methods · 实践方法Methods
1
当天睡前回顾——学习后 8-12 小时内做一次主动回忆，记忆留存率可提升一倍以上。睡眠中大脑会主动重组和巩固记忆。Review before sleep — Active recall 8-12 hours after learning more than doubles retention. During sleep, the brain actively reorganizes and consolidates memories.
2
用闪卡工具自动化——Anki、RemNote 等工具内置间隔算法，自动安排最佳复习时间。Use spaced repetition software — Anki, RemNote, etc. have built-in algorithms that schedule optimal review intervals automatically.
3
先自测，再翻书——强迫大脑在"困难模式"下提取信息，哪怕错了，学习效果也比直接看答案好。Test yourself first, then check — Forcing your brain to retrieve in "hard mode," even if wrong, is more effective than immediately seeing the answer.
4
交错练习不同主题——不要一次只学一个章节，混着复习。大脑在切换中建立更牢固的连接。Interleave different topics — Don't study one chapter at a time; mix reviews. The brain builds stronger connections through switching.
5
睡前 1 小时停止新学习——睡眠中海马体会将缓存"写入"皮层长期存储。新信息会干扰这一巩固过程。Stop new learning 1 hour before sleep — During sleep, the hippocampus "writes" cached memories to cortical long-term storage. New information interferes with this consolidation.
