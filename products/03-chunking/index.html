The same 18 letters: a novice sees meaningless symbols, a frequent flyer sees 6 international airports (New York · London · Paris · Tokyo · Beijing · Sydney). Working memory capacity hasn't changed, but the information density per chunk differs by 3×.

II. OBSERVATIONS · 观察 OBSERVATIONS · Observations
Fig. 2
Obs. 1
组块是「意义的容器」——不是任意分组，而是基于已有知识的结构化压缩。电话号码 138-0013-8000 比 13800138000 好记，因为前者符合「区段」模式。 Chunks are "containers of meaning" — not arbitrary grouping, but structured compression based on prior knowledge. A phone number 138-0013-8000 is easier than 13800138000 because the former fits the "segment" pattern.
Obs. 2
专家看「森林」，新手看「树木」——国际象棋大师能记住 20+ 个棋子的位置，不是因为记忆力更好，而是因为棋局符合他们的「模式库」。随机摆放的棋子，大师和新手记住的数量差不多。 Experts see the forest, novices see the trees — Chess masters remember 20+ piece positions not because of better memory, but because positions fit their "pattern library." With random arrangements, masters and novices perform equally.
Obs. 3
组块化是「可逆的」——一个组块可以被拆解为子组块，也可以被压缩进更大的组块。字母 → 单词 → 短语 → 概念框架，每一层都是上一层的组块化。 Chunking is reversible — A chunk can be unpacked into sub-chunks or compressed into a larger chunk. Letters → words → phrases → conceptual frameworks; each level is a chunking of the previous.
Obs. 4
自动化是组块化的终点——当组块被重复调用数千次后，它从「意识加工」变成「自动执行」。这就是为什么熟练司机可以不假思索地换挡，而新手需要思考每一步。 Automation is the endpoint of chunking — After thousands of repetitions, a chunk shifts from "conscious processing" to "automatic execution." This is why experienced drivers shift gears without thinking, while novices deliberate each step.
III. METHODS · 实践方法 METHODS · Practical Methods
Fig. 3
1
寻找模式，而非记忆细节——学吉他时，不要记「第2弦第3品」，而是记「C和弦形状」。模式一旦建立，细节自然跟随。 Look for patterns, not details — When learning guitar, don't memorize "2nd string, 3rd fret"; memorize the "C chord shape." Once the pattern is established, details follow naturally.
2
用「大纲」给信息分层——一个组块需要 7±2 个连接才能稳定（Miller, 1956）。给新知识建立层级结构（总-分-例），就是把零散信息压缩成可携带的组块。 Use outlines to layer information — A chunk needs 7±2 connections to stabilize (Miller, 1956). Creating hierarchical structure (general → specific → example) compresses scattered information into portable chunks.
3
把新知识「挂载」到旧组块上——学习不是从零开始，而是在已有模式上扩展。教一个懂围棋的人下象棋，比教完全新手快得多。 Mount new knowledge onto old chunks — Learning doesn't start from zero; it extends existing patterns. Teaching chess to a Go player is far faster than teaching a complete beginner.
4
刻意练习到自动化——同一个组块重复调用数千次后，它会从「占用工作记忆」变成「自动执行」。这是从「刻意练习」到「流畅表达」的唯一路径。 Deliberate practice until automatic — After calling the same chunk thousands of times, it shifts from "occupying working memory" to "automatic execution." This is the only path from deliberate practice to fluent expression.
5
教授他人是最好的组块化测试——如果你无法把一个概念用简单语言讲清楚，说明它还没有被真正组块化。费曼技巧的本质是「压缩验证」。 Teaching others is the best chunking test — If you can't explain a concept in simple terms, it hasn't been truly chunked. The Feynman Technique is essentially "compression verification."

* 核心机制：组块化通过压缩信息减少工作记忆负荷，让有限缓存处理更大问题 —— 这是专家思维的本质。

* Core mechanism: Chunking reduces working memory load by compressing information, allowing limited cache to handle larger problems — this is the essence of expert thinking.
